In practice

What ownership looks like day to day.

Getting data in does not sign it away. Importing a spreadsheet, syncing a mailbox or collecting signups through a public form gives us no rights to that data beyond running the service for you. It stays yours, under your workspace, subject to your instructions.

Volunteers see slices, never the list. A canvasser gets the turf they are walking; a driver gets the route they are driving. Nobody hands a whole voter file to a phone in the field, so a lost phone is an inconvenience, not a breach of your list.

Leaving is a button, not a negotiation. Export everything to CSV from settings, then delete your workspace. Deletion runs after a 30-day grace window that protects you from a bad day, not us; cancel it any time before it runs. We do not do retention calls, exit fees or deliberately awkward export formats.

We compete on the product, not on lock-in. Your newsletters carry your organization’s name, address and sending domain, not ours. Your engagement data is never rented to platforms or pooled across customers. A tool you can leave in an afternoon has to keep earning your business every month, and that is the point.

The technical side of these promises, from workspace isolation to encryption, lives on the security page.
